THE OPPORTUNITY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

What is Hyperliquid?
Hyperliquid is a fully on-chain order book perpetual futures exchange. Unlike AMM-based perp DEXs, it uses a traditional limit order book model that provides CEX-like trading experience with DeFi's self-custody benefits. The protocol built its own L1 (HyperBFT consensus) specifically optimized for trading, achieving 100k+ TPS with sub-second finality.

The Market
The perpetual futures market is the largest segment in crypto trading, with centralized exchanges processing $50-100B+ daily. On-chain perps represent <5% of this volume but are growing rapidly as traders seek non-custodial alternatives post-FTX. Hyperliquid's main competitors include GMX, dYdX, and Drift, but HL has captured dominant market share through superior UX.

THE PROTOCOL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

How It Works
- Custom L1 blockchain (HyperBFT) optimized for trading
- Fully on-chain order book (not off-chain matching)
- Sub-second block times, ~100k TPS capacity
- Native bridge from Arbitrum (USDC deposits)
- HLP (Hyperliquid Liquidity Provider) vault for market making
- Spot trading launched via HIP-1 token standard

Competitive Advantage
1. Performance: Only perp DEX with true CEX-like speed
2. No VC: Community-owned, no insider dumps
3. Full stack: Own L1 means no gas competition
4. UX: Clean interface, one-click trading
5. Network effects: Liquidity begets liquidity

RISKS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Smart Contract / Bridge Risk (MEDIUM)
Custom L1 and bridge are novel code. Any exploit could drain funds.

Regulatory Risk (HIGH)
Perp DEXs are under increasing scrutiny. US users technically restricted.

Competition Risk (MEDIUM)
dYdX v4 and other L1 perp chains are launching. Network effects matter.

Centralization Risk (MEDIUM)
Validator set is still small. Team has significant influence.

Market Risk (HIGH)
Perp volumes correlate with crypto volatility. Bear markets = lower fees.
