THE OPPORTUNITY
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

What is Pendle?
Pendle enables the tokenization and trading of future yield. Users can:
- Earn FIXED yield by buying Principal Tokens (PT) at a discount
- Speculate on VARIABLE yield by buying Yield Tokens (YT)
- Provide liquidity in Pendle's AMM pools designed for time-decay assets

The protocol wraps yield-bearing assets (stETH, aUSDC, etc.) and splits them into:
- PT (Principal Token): Redeemable 1:1 for underlying at maturity
- YT (Yield Token): Receives all yield until maturity

The Market
- Total DeFi TVL: ~$100B globally

- Yield tokenization TAM: Potentially multi-trillion as DeFi/TradFi convergence accelerates
- Pendle market share: Dominant in yield tokenization vertical (~90%+)
- Key competitors: Notional (fixed-rate lending), Element Finance (deprecated)

THE PROTOCOL
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

How It Works
1. Standardized Yield (SY): Wraps yield-bearing tokens into standard interface
2. PT/YT Split: SY tokens split into Principal and Yield components
3. AMM: Custom constant-product AMM with time-decay curve (converges PT → underlying at maturity)
4. Markets: Each pool has specific maturity date (e.g., Dec 2026 stETH)

Key Innovation: The AMM is designed specifically for assets that decay toward a known value over time, solving the unique challenge of trading yield.

Competitive Advantage
- First mover in yield tokenization with production-grade product
- Custom AMM designed for time-decay assets (no other protocol has this)
- Multi-chain deployment (8+ chains including new chains like Sonic, Berachain)
- Strong integrations with major LST/LRT protocols (Lido, Renzo, Etherfi, etc.)
- New Boros product: First on-chain perpetual funding rate tokenization

RISKS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Smart Contract Risk (MEDIUM)
Pendle V2 has been live since 2023 with no major exploits. However, complexity of PT/YT mechanics and AMM creates larger attack surface than simple protocols.

Competition Risk (LOW-MEDIUM)
Currently dominant, but yield tokenization is a large opportunity. Bigger players could enter. However, Pendle's AMM innovation is hard to replicate.

Liquidity Risk (MEDIUM)
Thin liquidity for longer-dated markets. A Medium article noted $8M liquidity in Sep 2026 pool with 5% slippage on $100K trade. Scaling to institutional sizes remains a challenge.

Token Transition Risk (LOW)
vePENDLE → sPENDLE transition is complex. Execution risk during migration period. However, team has clear plan and timeline.

Yield Compression Risk (MEDIUM)
If DeFi yields compress significantly, demand for yield tokenization decreases. Boros (funding rate tokenization) helps diversify beyond traditional yields.
